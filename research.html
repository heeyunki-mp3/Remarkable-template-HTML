June 2023 - Current

Cyber Insurance

This project proposes a novel approach to automatically classify, analyze, and predict the behavior of incoming cyberattacks in real-time. By leveraging honeypots—deceptive systems designed to lure and observe attackers in controlled environments—valuable data is collected, providing real-time insights into the evolving tactics of attackers. This project deploys three high-interaction honeypots within Columbia University's network, strategically placed in different security zones. The collected data, including SSH commands and file transfers, is analyzed to cluster attack patterns and predict future actions using machine learning techniques such as agglomerative clustering and Markov Chain analysis. The analysis and evaluation is stil in process.

January 2024 - July 2024

IoT Firewall

IoT devices are susceptible to botnet malware due to inherent system limitations. Once compromised, these devices can be exploited to leak private user information or launch DDoS attacks. In this paper, we present Wital, an adaptable mitigation strategy that curtails the malicious use of IoT devices through a stringent whitelist-based approach. This method combines static manufacturer usage profiling and dynamic traffic monitoring into a client-side firewall. Our strategy ensures that, even if devices are compromised, their impact is minimized. Using the P4 language for programmable data planes, we prototype the firewall solutions and demonstrate that this solution can significantly reduce the potential device exploitation.

Privacy Budget

The goal of this research is to perverse privacy budget through a caching layer for linear query workloads over differential private databases. My role was to prototype and evaluate on real-world Citi Bike datasets on Grafana and Tableau show that the solution preserves more budget compared to former cache.
